Fire Sentry

Using Multi-Layer Perceptron and Random Tree Classifier machine learning models, we were able to predict the occurrence of wildfires across the United States. You can start typing any address into the Address Bar and it will bring up the autocomplete for it (alternatively, you can just use the latitude and longitude). It will then bring up an image for the specified area and show the chance of a wildfire in the area based on current satellite data and location.

Fetch Game

Utilizing Unity, I developed reinforcement learning algorithms to simulate animal behaviors and interactions within virtual environments. Python and Matplotlib facilitated my data analysis, enabling insightful evaluations of our models for ongoing enhancement. By generating a 3D mesh and extrapolating data from successful trials, I created a nearly infinite amount of training scenarios, binned by difficulty. This research culminated in a presentation to scientists and management at Janelia Research Campus and was showcased at the Janelia Simulated Bodies Conference in 2023.

Autonomous Blimp

I created a Pygame-based physics simulator for realistic blimp movement and interactions in a simulated environment. I then designed user-friendly interfaces to improve simulation control and user experience. This work presented its findings in an abstract published in the Journal of Student-Scientists' Research.
